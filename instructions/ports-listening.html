How to check ports in use / listening

Instruction: see which ports are listening and which process uses them

What this is

The server application listens on one or more ports. If the port is not listening, or another process has taken it, connections fail. You need to confirm the expected port is open and bound to the right process.

How to do it

  1. Linux: ss -tlnp or netstat -tlnp to list listening TCP ports and the process (PID). Check that your app’s port appears and the PID is your server process.
  2. Windows: netstat -ano to see listening ports and the owning PID; match the PID in Task Manager or Get-Process -Id <PID>.
  3. If the port is in use by another app, either stop that app, change your server’s config to use a different port, or resolve the conflict. If nothing is listening, the service may not have started—check service status and logs.
